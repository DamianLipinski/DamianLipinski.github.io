Case Study Prototypes About My Resume

Tearoom

Overview:

In 2018 I started to study Social Informatics and this project was the start of my UX designer journey. There was a lot of new knowledge to take in and put into practice. We came across a lot of challenges, but with proper organization and determination, we were able to handle them. Our work was exceptionally appreciated by our professors.

Firstly, we were trying to localize some problems that we are currently struggling with within our society. We put our focus on the feeling of loneliness within elderly people. Then we used different methods to find out why younger people don't reach so often to their parents or grandparents and we were trying to find a way to connect them.

One aspect of this problem was the fact, that we often seek help or advice on the internet, usually within our (not so experienced) peers. We proposed a platform, that allows sharing the stories and experience by older people, mostly with the assistance of younger people, who could handle technical issues and do the interview. We believe that would be also an incentive to hear out and spend some time with our parents and grandparents.

The problem

Our task was to localize some problems in day-to-day life. We decided to focus on the problem of intergenerational relations.

The role of elderlies in our society drastically changed due to technological advancement. A few decades ago, we would seek knowledge and advice from older people. But now, we are often looking for that knowledge on the internet, where information is often unreliable. We wanted to find a way to encourage people, to look up practical knowledge, based on experience, from elderlies once again.

Six hats method

In the beginning, we decided to use the six thinking hats method. It allowed us to look at this problem from different perspectives, but one at a time, so it allowed us to avoid confusion from too many angles crowding our thinking.

CATWOE analysis

Looking at the problem using the six hats method wasn't enough, because we wanted to look from the perspective of different stakeholders. This is when the CATWOE analysis came in handy. That method allowed us to see the impact our solution could have. At that time we fully understood how important our work is and how the tools we create can affect the people around us.

Customers

  • Who is on the receiving end? - The young people
  • What problem do they have now? - Lack of access to knowledge from the elderlies
  • How will they react to what you are proposing? - They will wonder about the benefits of using the method proposed by us. But in time they would see the advantage of gaining knowledge in that way
  • Who are the winners and losers? - The winners are younger and older people. The younger will have new space to gain knowledge, the older will be listened to and appreciated

Actors

  • Who are the people who will ‘do the doing’, carrying out your solution? - Younger people that are seeking someone experienced in life and older people that can share their knowledge
  • What is the impact on them? - Older people are feeling useless, and younger don't have a reliable source of practical knowledge based on life experience

Transformation

  • What is the process for transforming inputs into outputs? - On the input, there is a difficulty with passing the knowledge within generations. On the output, there is facilitate of the communication
  • What are all the steps in between?
    1. Making an interface
    2. Gathering the knowledge from elderlies
    3. Making adjustments, to be more accessible to the customers
    4. Making access to the gathered knowledge
    5. Using the interface

World-view

  • What is the bigger picture into which the situation fits? - The older people like to share their knowledge. When they can help others tell about their experiences, they feel needed
  • What is the real problem you are working on? - The younger and older people move away from each other, they have less and less contact

Owner

  • From who we are codependent? - Investors, customers
  • Who can help us? - Investors, people with relevant experience
  • Who can stop us? - Corporations, competition

Environmental constraints

  • What are the broader constraints that act on the situation and your ideas?
    • Technological: Limited resources and tools, like automatic transcription
    • Social: Younger people are used to their way of seeking knowledge and it can be hard to change these habits
  • What are the ethical limits, laws, financial constraints, limited resources, regulations, and so on? - We need to be careful not to cross the line of privacy
  • How might these constrain your solution? How can you get around them? - We can make a guideline of what is recommended and what we should not share

Value Proposition Canvas

After seeing the problem from different perspectives, we decided to have a better look at the customer. We did that to ensure that a product is positioned around what the customer values and needs. To do that we used Value Proposition Canvas

Qualitative Interviews

Finally, it was time to talk with potential customers. To do that, we designed qualitative interviews. Each member of our group conducted 2 such interviews, so we had 8 subjects in total. We decided to do the interviews with students, and to assure this group is diverse, each of them was a student of a different field. To gain more experience, one of the interview was recorded, and at the other, we took notes.

Results

We found out, that older people are still valuable to the young generation. They like to listen to and spend time with elderlies. But we don't need pieces of advice from them to start independent lives, because we can find a lot of helpful guides on the internet. So people don't reach out to ask about cleaning or cooking, but they find life lessons particularly helpful. Because they are based on the long-life experience they seem more credible than guidance on the internet.

Personas

After a longish analysis, we felt that we knew exactly what kind of user types might use our service. We understood the user's needs, experiences, behaviors, and goals. So it was time to create personas, to have that picture in mind through the rest of the process. We created two personas based on interviews - 19-year-old Matylda and 20-year-old Antoś.

Usage scenario and Storyboard

We created two usage scenarios. One is from the perspective of elderly Miss Jadzia, and the other is from the perspective of a student Antek. They represent two factors that complement each other. Jadzia is happy to share their stories and read how they positively impacted on lives of the people like Antek. Based on those stories we created a storyboard, that visualize both perspectives.

User stories

At this stage of the project, we started to think about how our platform could look like, and what elements or tools it should contain. We were thinking about the best and the most natural way to represent the features we want to implement. We decided to do that via user stories.

Information architecture

We were ready to start making the first sketches. But before that, we needed to think about information architecture. The main assumption was to make content creators as important as the content audience from the structure perspective. We wanted to do that, by emphasizing both parts to the same extent

Sketches

After the information structure was made, we started the process of making the sketches - we went from a lot of very basic ideas, to wireframe that result in the final prototype

Prototype

The final step in our project was building a low-fidelity prototype. We have build it using Balsamiq. We named our platform "tearoom", to build an association with feeling of warm, pleasure, and enjoyable time

Final thoughts

When we were working on this project, we started by localizing a problem, and we end up creating a prototype of the platform, that could be implemented and have a real impact.

I learned to look at the big picture of the process of delivering products. I learned also to look up real user problems, which I need to fully understand, to create the best possible service.

The work in an involved team showed me, how we can complement each other, despite having weaknesses in some areas.

Finally, I learned and put into practice a lot of useful methods and tools, that I can use in future projects.
