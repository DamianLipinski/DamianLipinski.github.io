Case Study Prototypes About My Resume

Biogo redesign

Disclaimer: I have no associations with Biogo. This project was made for educational purposes

Overview:

We were given a task to increase the conversion rate at the biogo website. Firstly, we have made a few usability tests to locate the most problematic elements. After analyzing the results, we did a competition analysis, with a focus on the elements that need some improvements on Biogo. Finally, we have designed a few problematic elements and we proposed the plan for further development.

Team:

3 members

Methods:

Usage testing, Benchmarking, Prototyping

Tools:

Figma

Timeline:

3 weeks, 2020

The problem

At the start of the project, we were given the following fictional e-mail, from the owner of the Biogo store:

Re: Conversion rate increase
Hello,
My biggest competition is https://minimal.com.pl . They are selling 10-12% more than us. I have to admit that I really like their site.
I am also interested in other websites related to the eco-lifestyle. The following pages seem inspiring to me: https://www.rajapack.co.uk/paper_vs_plastic.html https://www.nytimes.com/paidpost/allbirds/the-view-from-above.html https://drinkeverandever.com
I am counting on your help.
Best regards, Joanna Kwiecińska The owner


So the problem we were facing was redesigning a website so it would have more customers. Our client wanted to have a more minimalistic and intuitive store, as she had seen at the competitors. We were given the websites, that our client found interesting, and it gave us a sense of what kind of aesthetic she likes.

Usability tests

We spotted a few flaws right away, but we didn't want to jump to conclusions, so we decided to arrange three usability tests. Our respondents were people 20 to 30 years old - the main target of this store. We were able to localize the greatest advantages and the elements that need to be improved or rethought.

Visuals

The first thing our respondents noticed was the overall aesthetic of the website. They said that it looks modern and distinct from the other stores. The recommended products on the landing page was a good idea, as they got respondents attention right away. That's because they were new or in unfamiliar forms.

Product description

The product description page gave a good impression. It is clear, contains a lot of information, and allows easy navigation to delivery options and product reviews.

"About us" bookmark

Users also claimed that the "about us" bookmark is interestingly described and gives trust. You get to know the mission and commitment of the creators, which makes you want to stay on this site a little longer.

Contrast

The color selection makes a good impression at the beginning. But after some time of browsing a store, it becomes tiring and hard to read. We found out it was because of the poor contrast of the font and background color. Based on the contrast test we made the two main font colors got the 1.9 and 4.42 scores. According to WCAG, the minimum is the ratio of 4.1 and the standard should be 7.5. These results were reflected in our empirical test. Almost all of the users told us that the use of the website was tiring on the eyes.



Menu

An element that seemed to need some adjustments is the layout of the categories in the menu tab. After usability tests, we realized it needs to be changed completely. The users found it difficult to find a given category, they were unsure why some categories are on the main bar and the "menu" tab and whether they were the same category. The menu was difficult to navigate, as users often closed the tab accidentally, which forced them to navigate more carefully. Some of the categories are too long, which causes a lot of scrolling.

Filters

The filters are often used to narrowing given products. The available filters on biogo are quite understandable. Users didn't have a problem finding the option they needed, but using them is tedious and discouraging. That is because of a few reasons. Every time we select a filter, the whole page refreshes. So selecting a few filters causes refreshing several times. Additionally, we don't see selected filters right away. To see if a given filter is selected we need to click on that filter. Checking every one of many filters to see which of them are selected is very time-consuming.

Benchmark - Competition analysis

On competition analysis, we were looking at the stores that are similar to Biogo. We focused on the elements that Biogo didn't handle very well.

Minimal

The first store we decided to analyze was the suggested Minimal store. Their strengths are minimalism and clarity. They resigned from more common filters and used tags instead. A lot of design choices were possible due to the selected offer, without as many products as Biogo has. The things that Biogo lacks is simplicity and readability.



Betterland

On this website, we noticed how the menu is made. They have a lot of categories, just like Biogo, but it is better thought out and make the navigation much easier.



Suggested solutions

After usability tests and competition analysis, we started to think about the solutions. We proposed a few of the solutions that could be implemented.

Contrast

When dealing with contrast we knew, that chosen color has to be similar to the previous one and be much better readable. A good contrast ratio is crucial, as it will give a better visual reception of the portal, and customers will have a better overall experience. Our proposition got an 8.7 ratio.



Menu

We decided to make the menu clearer and better organized. Now it has two main categories - grocery and drugstore. We carefully planned the subcategories in order not to make any confusion.



Categories submenu layout

The categories submenu was too long making filters hard to find. We decided to show just the current section, with the option to go back to more general sections.



Filters

We made the filters more responsive and readable. It will make browsing the store easier. Simplification of the customer's route on the side can translate into more transactions.



Final thoughts

With this project, I learned how the real contacts with a client could look like, and what to expect. We couldn't do anything, because we had to meet the client's needs, but we also had to show initiative.

Usability tests helped us to see how the real scenario interaction looks like, and to spot the elements that confuse users. We also get feedback on the overall experience.

Benchmarking was an important step, as we saw how the other stores look like before we started redesigning elements. It is crucial to design something, that is familiar to the user and easy to navigate, but also has distinct look.

In the last step, we have redesigned some elements based on previous research. We believe that the changes we made, would make this website more user-friendly, which would result in higher conversion.
